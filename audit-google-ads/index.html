Get Access First

You need Google Ads (admin), GA4 (editor), Google Tag Manager, and Merchant Center (if e-commerce). Without full access, you're auditing blind. Also pull last 90 days of data as your baseline.

02

Work the Steps In Order

Tracking always goes first. If your measurement is broken, every other finding is meaningless — you'll be optimizing against wrong signals. Fix tracking, then audit structure, then bidding.

01
Foundation — Do This First
Conversion Tracking
If this is wrong, everything else is a waste of time · ~60 min
Critical +
⚠
The #1 problem in Google Ads audits: accounts that look fine but are optimizing against the wrong signal. Soft conversions (page views, scroll depth, add-to-cart) set as primary conversions will train Smart Bidding to find more of those — not buyers. Fix this before touching anything else.
  • Primary conversion is revenue-correlated only — purchase, lead form submit, booked call, or phone lead. Nothing softer.2026 Rule
  • Secondary conversions are set to "observation only" — page views, add-to-cart, time on site. They inform; they don't drive bidding.
  • No duplicate conversions — verify GA4 event and Google Ads tag are NOT both counting the same action as primary. Cross-reference Ads conversion count vs. backend order count for same 30-day period.Common Bug
  • Thank-you page tag fires once, on correct URL — use Tag Assistant to verify. It should fire exactly once per conversion, not on every page load.
  • Enhanced conversions — unified toggle enabled — as of April 2026, web and lead enhanced conversions are one setting. Turn it on. Accepts both tag and API data simultaneously.April 2026
  • Server-side conversion API active or on the roadmap — client-side tags miss 15–40% of conversions due to browser blocking and ITP. Server-side is the 2026 standard.Best Practice
  • UTM parameters consistent across 10–20 destination URLs — check that auto-tagging isn't conflicting with manual UTMs. Inconsistencies break cross-channel attribution.
  • Phone call conversions use a real conversion event — not just a click-to-call. CallTrackingMetrics, CallRail, or Google's forwarding number is required for real call tracking.
  • Offline conversion import (OCI) set up if you have a sales cycle — lead gen businesses must close the loop by importing CRM-qualified leads back into Google Ads.2026 Priority
  • Consent Mode V2 implemented — from June 2026, ad_storage consent controls Ads data independently of GA4. Non-compliance = lost signal for Smart Bidding in GDPR markets.June 2026
  • Conversion window matches your sales cycle — 30-day click window is default. If your sales cycle is 90 days, extend it. If it's 24 hours (impulse purchase), shorten it.
  • Data-driven attribution selected — last-click is legacy. DDA uses all touchpoints. Only revert to last-click if you have fewer than 300 conversions/month and DDA is unstable.2026 Standard
→
Quick sanity check: Pull 30-day conversion count from Google Ads. Pull order/lead count from your CRM or Shopify for the same period. If Ads shows 2× more conversions, you have a double-firing or weak-signal problem. Fix this before step 2.
02
Architecture
Campaign Structure
Brand vs. non-brand · AI Max · DSA migration · ~45 min
Critical +
⚠
2026 structural shift: The old keyword-first architecture is being replaced by intent-signal architecture. AI Max and PMax both require you to think in terms of inputs to the algorithm (assets, audiences, negative lists, brand exclusions) rather than a tightly controlled keyword list.
  • Brand campaign is separated from non-brand — mixing them inflates apparent ROAS. Brand traffic converts cheap and makes non-brand look worse. Always separate.
  • Brand terms excluded from PMax and AI Max campaigns — PMax will steal brand traffic and attribute conversions to itself. Add brand exclusions at account level.Critical PMax Rule
  • Campaign goals align with actual business KPIs — a lead gen campaign set to "maximize clicks" will get clicks. A revenue campaign set to "maximize conversions" (not value) will find cheap conversions, not high-value ones.
  • DSA campaigns audited for AI Max migration readiness — DSAs auto-upgrade to AI Max in September 2026. Audit now: what queries are your DSAs matching? Do you have a negative list ready?Sept 2026 Deadline
  • AI Max — brand exclusions and URL restrictions configured — AI Max can generate ad copy from your site and match to expanded queries. Without guardrails, it will go off-brand and off-target.AI Max
  • Call-only campaigns flagged for RSA migration — new call-only ads blocked since February 2026. Stop serving February 2027. Transition to RSAs + call assets now.Deprecated
  • eCPC bid strategy removed from all campaigns — largely deprecated in 2026. Replace with tCPA, tROAS, or Maximize Conversions depending on your conversion volume.Deprecated
  • Campaigns limited by budget are not running Smart Bidding — a throttled budget starves the algorithm of impressions and data. Either increase budget or switch to manual/tCPA with lower targets.
  • Ad schedule is set intentionally — from June 2026, budget pacing changes to target monthly limit (30.4× daily budget) regardless of schedule. Active schedule days still matter for when ads show.
  • Geographic targeting matches your actual service area — review geo reports. "People in or regularly in" vs. "People searching for" is a common misconfiguration for local service businesses.
Old 2019–2023 Structure
  • Exact match keyword control at campaign level
  • DSA as catch-all for long-tail
  • Manual bids or eCPC
  • Brand and non-brand mixed together
  • Campaign per keyword theme
2026 Signal Architecture
  • Inputs to AI: assets, audiences, negative lists
  • AI Max or PMax with tight guardrails
  • Smart Bidding with clean conversion signal
  • Always separate brand from non-brand
  • Campaign per margin band or product category
03
Query Control
Keywords & Negatives
Search term waste · match type discipline · negative lists · ~60 min
High +
⚠
2026 reality: Exact match isn't exact. Phrase match isn't phrase. Google's intent-matching means match types are now inputs into a signal system — not precision filters. Your negative keyword list is your primary defence against waste.
  • Account-level negative keyword list exists with 1,000 entries max — brand terms on non-brand campaigns, irrelevant product categories, and known junk queries. This is your first line of defence.
  • Search term report reviewed in last 14 days — pull 90 days. Sort by cost. Find queries that spent money but drove zero conversions. Add them as negatives immediately.
  • Competitor brand terms are intentional — either you're bidding on competitors (and that's a deliberate strategy with a dedicated campaign) or you're not, and they're blocked as negatives.
  • Informational queries excluded — "how to", "free", "DIY", "what is", "YouTube tutorial for" should be negatives unless you're running awareness campaigns targeting them intentionally.Waste Alert
  • Broad match keywords have a negative list backing them — never run broad match without a managed negative list. Broad match + no negatives = wasted spend at scale.Non-Negotiable
  • Low-performing keywords paused or removed — keywords with 100+ clicks and zero conversions in 90 days. Either the keyword is irrelevant or the landing page is broken.
  • Quality Score below 5 flagged for repair — QS 7+ is healthy. Below 5 signals ad relevance, expected CTR, or landing page experience issues. Don't just add negatives — fix the ad.
  • AI Max search theme list reviewed — search themes in AI Max act like broad match signals. Audit what themes are active. Are they aligned with your actual intent?AI Max
  • Negative keywords applied to Display and PMax — display targeting uses content matching, not keywords — but placement exclusions and topic negatives matter. PMax now accepts account-level negatives via the UI.PMax UI Change
  • Single keyword ad groups (SKAGs) removed — SKAGs were built for a world where match types worked like filters. They're outdated. Consolidate into theme-based ad groups with 5–15 keywords.
→
Operator tip from Hans: Sort your search term report by spend descending, filter for zero conversions. The top 20 rows will often reveal 15–30% of your budget going to garbage. This is the fastest single fix in most accounts. Add those terms as exact negatives before anything else.
04
Bid Strategy
Smart Bidding & Budget
tCPA · tROAS · learning phase · budget pacing · ~45 min
High +
→
Smart Bidding is a contract. You give it clean conversion data, a realistic target, and enough budget to operate. It finds the conversions. Break any part of that contract — bad data, aspirational ROAS, throttled budget — and it fails. The algorithm isn't wrong. The inputs are.
  • Every campaign has a bid strategy appropriate to its goal — lead gen: tCPA or Maximize Conversions. E-commerce with margin data: tROAS or Maximize Conversion Value. Brand: Target Impression Share or Manual. Awareness: Maximize Clicks (only acceptable here).
  • tCPA and tROAS targets are based on historical data — not aspirational guesses — pull 90-day average CPA. Set tCPA no lower than 20% below that. Setting it 50% below actual performance starves the algorithm.
  • Smart Bidding campaigns have 30+ conversions in past 30 days — below this, the algorithm has insufficient data. Use Maximize Conversions (no target) until you hit the threshold, then layer in tCPA.Minimum Signal
  • Campaigns are not stuck in learning phase — check the status column. "Learning" for more than 7 days indicates conversion signal issues, budget throttling, or recent structural changes resetting the model.
  • Maximize Clicks is not used on conversion campaigns — this strategy optimizes for traffic volume, not quality. Only acceptable for new brand campaigns with no conversion history or pure awareness.Legacy Setting
  • eCPC (enhanced CPC) removed from all campaigns — deprecated in 2026. It's a half-manual, half-automated hybrid that underperforms both manual and full Smart Bidding. Remove it.Deprecated
  • Conversion value rules are used if you have different customer segments — weight high-LTV customers, high-margin products, or new vs. returning customers differently. This tells Smart Bidding what to actually optimize for.2026 Best Practice
  • Budget pacing change acknowledged — June 2026 — from June 1, pacing targets monthly limit (30.4× daily) instead of active schedule days. Budgets may pace differently. Monitor daily spend closely after this change.June 2026
  • Bid strategy experiments are documented — if you're testing tCPA vs. Maximize Conversions, the experiment needs a hypothesis, start date, end date, and success metric. Undocumented experiments teach you nothing.
  • Shared budgets reviewed — shared budgets can cause one campaign to starve another. Review which campaigns share budgets and whether that allocation is intentional.
05
Automated Campaigns
Performance Max Audit
Asset groups · audience signals · brand exclusions · channel segmentation · ~90 min
High +
⚠
PMax in 2026: More transparent than before (asset experiments now live, better channel reporting), but still a fog machine without the right inputs. The four changes that recover 10–20% of PMax spend: account-level brand exclusions, search term insights review cadence, asset group naming convention, and a dedicated campaign per product margin band.
  • Asset group Ad Strength is "Excellent" or "Good" — "Poor" or "Average" rated asset groups actively drag campaign performance. Replace low-rated assets immediately. "Low" rated assets don't just underperform — they penalize.Direct Impact
  • Full asset coverage across all types — minimum: 5 headlines, 5 descriptions, 5 images (landscape + square + portrait), 1 logo, 1 video. Missing video? Google auto-generates one. It will be off-brand.Critical
  • Asset group experiments set up — as of April 2026, you can now A/B test assets inside PMax. Use this. It's the first real creative testing capability PMax has had.April 2026
  • Audience signals include Customer Match list — upload your existing customer list. This is the single highest-quality audience signal you can give PMax. Refresh it at least monthly.Non-Negotiable
  • Audience signals include high-LTV converters segment — not just "all visitors" — "all website visitors" is a weak signal. Segment by purchase history, AOV, or product category. Give PMax your best customers, not everyone.
  • Brand exclusions configured at account level — PMax will cannibalize your brand search traffic and attribute those conversions to itself. Block brand terms. Dedicated brand Search campaign captures that traffic cheaper.Must Do
  • PMax is NOT mixing all products in one campaign — segment by product margin band or category. A PMax campaign mixing $20 products with $2,000 products will optimize toward volume, not revenue.
  • Final URLs are controlled — not drifting to weak pages — check URL expansion settings. By default, PMax can send traffic to any page on your site. Lock it to your best-converting landing pages unless you've explicitly audited URL performance.
  • Channel-level performance segmented via Insights or MCC API — PMax blends Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discovery. Pull channel breakdown from the Insights report. If Display is at $95 CPA and Search is at $30 CPA, the blended number is hiding the waste.
  • Brand guidelines are applied to PMax campaigns — auto-migrated by October 2025 for existing campaigns. Verify your brand colors, fonts, and logo are correctly set and haven't been overwritten.Post-Migration Check
  • Your Data Exclusions feature reviewed — new in 2026: you can exclude specific first-party segments from PMax targeting. Use this to exclude current customers from acquisition campaigns if your goal is new customer revenue only.New 2026
  • AI-generated assets reviewed for brand voice accuracy — Google's AI can generate ad copy from your site. Review it. It often pulls from FAQ pages or footer text. Approve only what's on-brand and accurate.
→
Hans's PMax rule: PMax is only as good as your conversion signal quality. If it's optimizing toward junk leads because your tracking is misconfigured, it will scale junk leads. Step 1 is not optional. Fix tracking, then give PMax the right audience inputs. In that order.
06
Ad Creative
RSAs, Assets & Ad Copy
RSA performance ratings · pinning strategy · asset coverage · ~45 min
Medium +
  • Every active ad group has at least one RSA — Expanded Text Ads (ETAs) have been deprecated since June 2022. If you still have ETAs only in an ad group, add an RSA immediately.
  • RSA ad strength is "Good" or "Excellent" — "Average" or "Poor" rated RSAs signal weak asset variety, repetitive headlines, or missing assets. Google reduces impression share for low-rated ads.
  • Pinning is used sparingly and intentionally — pinning locks a headline into a position, reducing Google's ability to test combinations. Only pin when legally or compliance-required. Too many pins = lower reach.
  • 15 headlines and 4 descriptions filled per RSA — max capacity. Fewer assets = fewer combinations = lower Ad Strength. Every slot should be a genuinely different message angle.
  • Best-performing asset combinations identified — use the "Combinations" report inside RSA performance. What headlines and descriptions Google is actually pairing. Double down on what's working.2026 Report
  • All available ad assets (extensions) are active — sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, call, lead form, price, promotion, image. Missing assets = smaller ad real estate = lower CTR = higher effective CPC. Full assets can improve CTR 10–15%.
  • Real-time policy review — Real-Time Policy Reviews enabled — as of April 2026, RSAs get instant policy feedback during creation (seconds vs. hours). Check your ads aren't waiting hours on policy review when this feature can clear them instantly.April 2026
  • Ad copy includes specific numbers, proof points, and calls to action — generic ads ("We're the best!") lose to specific ads ("53% lower CPA in 60 days — free audit"). Review headlines for specificity.
  • AI-generated copy reviewed for brand voice compliance — Google's text customization tools can now receive brand voice guidelines. Configure this. Review any AI-suggested copy before enabling.2025–2026
  • Ads reference the Search Partner Network performance report — Google released partner-level impression data in 2025 (in Report Editor under Content Suitability). Check if Search Partner traffic is performing or drag down your averages.2025 Feature
07
First-Party Data
Audiences & Customer Data
Customer Match · remarketing · consent mode · lookalikes · ~45 min
High +
→
2026 reality: Third-party audience signals are degrading. First-party data — your customer list, CRM data, and consented website behaviour — is now the primary competitive moat. Accounts feeding rich first-party data to Smart Bidding outperform accounts relying on Google's default audience targeting.
  • Customer Match list uploaded and refreshed monthly — this is the highest-quality audience signal available. Your hashed email list tells Google exactly who your best customers are. Stale lists over 90 days old lose match rate.Top Priority
  • Remarketing audiences are segmented — not just "all visitors" — separate: purchased, abandoned cart, visited product page, visited blog only. Each segment has different intent and value. Don't bid the same on all of them.
  • High-LTV customer segment built and uploaded — if you have CRM data, export your top 20% of customers by lifetime value and upload as a Customer Match list. Give Smart Bidding a picture of who to find more of.First-Party Moat
  • Audience signals in PMax asset groups include converters segment — don't lump all remarketing into one signal. Converters, high-LTV, and product page visitors should each be separate signals in separate asset groups if volume allows.
  • Consent Mode V2 implemented and verified — from June 15, 2026, ad_storage consent controls Ads data independently. Denying = Smart Bidding loses user identifiers beyond URL data. This affects audiences, attribution, and bidding signal quality.June 2026
  • GA4 properly linked to Google Ads and passing audience data — GA4 audiences (engaged users, purchasers, high-value segments) should be available in Google Ads audience manager. A disconnected GA4 = missing audience data for both targeting and Smart Bidding.
  • Your Data Exclusions reviewed in PMax — new in 2026: exclude existing customers from PMax to focus acquisition spend only on new users. Crucial if your goal is new customer revenue, not repeat purchase volume.New 2026
  • Low-performing audience segments identified and excluded or adjusted — review audience performance in Search campaigns (observation mode). If a segment has 10× worse CPA, exclude it or add a negative bid adjustment.
08
Measurement
Attribution & Incrementality
DDA · GA4 linkage · incrementality baseline · ROAS reality check · ~60 min
Foundation +
⚠
ROAS is not profit. Google Ads reports all attributed conversions as though they were caused by the ad. A significant portion of brand search and retargeting conversions are users who would have converted anyway. Your reported ROAS is almost certainly higher than your incremental ROAS. Measurement is how you find the truth.
  • Data-driven attribution (DDA) is selected — last-click attribution ignores all non-last-touch interactions. DDA distributes credit across the full path. It requires 300+ conversions/month — if you're below that, use position-based as an interim.2026 Standard
  • GA4 linked to Google Ads and data flowing correctly — check that GA4 conversions are visible inside Google Ads. A disconnected or misconfigured GA4 means you're missing cross-channel data and audience signal quality degrades.
  • Blended ROAS vs. incremental ROAS gap estimated — pull brand search conversion volume separately. If brand search represents 40% of your conversions, your non-brand ROAS is significantly lower than the blended number suggests. Know both figures.
  • Cross-campaign cannibalization checked — if Search and PMax/AI Max are both serving on the same high-intent queries, compare CPA by query. If PMax is serving at a higher CPC on the same queries as Search, the bundled campaign is cannibalizing a cheaper channel.
  • Incrementality experiment framework set up or planned — geo holdout tests (pause campaigns in test markets, keep in control markets) are the only way to measure true incremental conversions. Not required monthly, but at least once per year.Advanced
  • Google's Meridian MMM tool explored for larger accounts — Google launched Meridian in 2025 as an open-source Marketing Mix Model. For accounts over $50K/month, it provides incrementality measurement at channel level across paid and organic.2025 Launch
  • Ads Advisor alerts reviewed — launched January 2026, Ads Advisor proactively surfaces policy violations, security issues, and optimization opportunities. Check it. Don't let it sit unread.Jan 2026
  • Auto-applied recommendations reviewed and locked down — Google auto-applies recommendations unless you opt out. Review the auto-apply settings monthly. New recommendation types get added regularly and can change your account without warning.Watch This
  • Ads in AI Overviews eligibility checked — Google expanded AI Overview ads to desktop and global markets in 2025. If you're in a supported market, your campaigns may already be serving here. Review performance and opt out if the traffic quality is poor.2025 Expansion
